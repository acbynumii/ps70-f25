1. Box Design

For the design of the box, I used Fusion. Fusion is straightforward to use, and this process was made simple through parameterizing the finger joints (see this video). After completing the design, I exported the sketches and faces in DXF format to prepare it for further processing in Rhino. Next, the method of fabrication was laser-cutting, and I incorporated scoring, cutting, and rastering in the process. After the laser cutter was done, I hot glued the pieces together to make the result below.

2. Household Items

The process for designing these was fairly simple. First, you measure the dimensions of each object. For the cologne, it was a lot more challenging just because there was a lot of curves involved, but for the Expo marker, I used a lot of arcs and angles as well as the revolve feature. As for the cologne, there was a lot of cut features used in addition to the project function. Lastly, I made separate components for each assembly: the top for the expo marker and the top for the cologne. Constraining them included making the cylindrical holes in each piece concentric. For the cologne, I also had to align the faces such that top was facing the correct direction.
